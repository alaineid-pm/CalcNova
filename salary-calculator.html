Frequently Asked Questions
How is the annual salary calculated from an hourly rate?
Annual salary = hourly rate × hours per week × weeks per year. For a standard 40-hour, 52-week schedule: $28.85/hr × 40 × 52 = $60,000. Change the hours or weeks fields above to match your actual schedule.
What is the difference between biweekly and semimonthly pay?
Biweekly pay means you are paid every two weeks — 26 times per year. Semimonthly pay means you are paid twice a month — 24 times per year. This calculator uses biweekly (26 payments), which is the more common payroll frequency in the US.
How is the daily pay rate calculated?
Daily rate = hourly rate × (hours per week ÷ 5). This assumes a 5-day work week. For a standard 8-hour day: $28.85/hr × 8 = $230.77/day. If you work a compressed schedule (e.g., 4×10), adjust your hours per week accordingly.
Is the tax estimate accurate?
The tax estimate is a simplified calculation — it multiplies your gross pay by your entered tax rate and subtracts the result. Real-world taxes depend on your filing status, deductions, credits, FICA taxes, state/local taxes, and more. For accurate tax planning, consult a tax professional or use a dedicated tax calculator.
What does "weeks per year" mean and why would I change it?
By default, a full year is 52 weeks. If you take unpaid time off — for example, 2 weeks of unpaid leave — you would set weeks per year to 50. This affects the annual total for hourly workers who are only paid when they work. Salaried employees typically receive the same annual amount regardless of time off.
